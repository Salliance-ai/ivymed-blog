Why Seattle Tech Employees Are Ghosting Their Doctors

It's not avoidance. It's friction. The administrative burden of traditional psychiatric care is driving high-functioning professionals away from treatment.

March 22, 2026·5 min read·Dr. Ivy Jones, PMHNP-BC DNP

Your last psychiatrist required a 45-minute commute, a 30-minute wait, and a 20-minute appointment that ended with a voicemail about your prescription refill. You didn't ghost them because you don't care about your mental health. You ghosted them because the friction cost exceeded the perceived benefit.

The Friction Audit

Every touchpoint in traditional psychiatric care adds friction: phone-only scheduling during business hours, in-person intake requirements, 2-week insurance verification, paper fax intake forms, and voicemail-only prescription refills.

You didn't fail the system. The system failed to meet you where you are.

Zero-Friction Psychiatric Care

Your appointment is a video call. Your intake form is a link. Your prescription is electronic. Your provider responds via secure message. Your insurance is verified before you ever pick up the phone. This is not a premium tier — it's the baseline that modern telehealth makes possible.
